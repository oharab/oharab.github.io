# Session 1 — Thursday 30 April 2026

**Phase:** Rehab / movement screen  
**Goal:** Baseline screen, identify knee and diastasis limits, establish starting loads, get first feel for gymnastics-specific patterns  
**Duration:** 60–75 min  
**Stop if:** Knee pain > 3/10, any sharp or locking sensation, diastasis doming/coning, or breathing distress

---

## Warm-up — 10 min

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Notes | Demo | Done |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Diaphragmatic breathing | 1 × 10 breaths | Lying on back, knees bent. Breathe into belly and sides — not chest. Sets the tone for diastasis work. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5AFchTB2eXE | [x] |
| Cat-cow | 1 × 10 | Slow, full range. Note any stiffness or restriction. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xyNwxiuERXc | [x] |
| Cobra / press-up to cobra | 1 × 8 | Prone. Press up through hands, hips stay down. Assess spinal extension range and any lower back tightness. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=d1gLI6uLX-8 | [ ] |
| Hip 90/90 switch | 1 × 10 each side | Sit on floor, both legs at 90°. Rotate side to side. Note any hip restriction. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qq_Z7sAmVrA | [ ] |
| Hollow body hold (bent knee) | 2 × 10 sec | Lower back pressed into floor, knees drawn in, arms by sides or overhead. Note whether you can maintain pressure without bracing hard. Relevant to diastasis — stop if doming occurs. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lUtxvHizvsw | [ ] |
| Calf raise (bilateral, slow) | 1 × 15 | Bodyweight only. On a gymnastics block or step if available. Assess calf/ankle stiffness. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HmgXnST4Mdw | [ ] |

## Block 1 — Glute & hip screen (20 min)

*Purpose: find out where the weak links are in the chain driving the knee tracking issue.*

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Load | Cues | Demo | Done |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Glute bridge | 2 × 10 | Bodyweight | Feet hip-width, drive through heels. Note if one glute fires less. Pause 2 sec at top. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NXwR65L7I-s | [ ] |
| Clamshell | 2 × 12 each side | Light band or bodyweight | Side-lying, feet together. Rotate top knee up without rolling the pelvis back. Note asymmetry. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=744LxA1VPtY | [ ] |
| Side-lying hip abduction | 2 × 12 each side | Bodyweight | Straight top leg, slight forward lean of the foot. No hip hike. Use a crash mat to lie on — more comfortable than the floor. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nt74N6ZwqAE | [ ] |
| Single-leg balance with hip hinge | 2 × 8 each side | Bodyweight | Stand on one leg, soft knee, hinge forward at the hip until mild hamstring pull. This is about balance and hinge pattern, not depth — use a wall or bar for light support if needed. Note right vs left stability difference. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0hxtMb6kcbs | [ ] |
| Ring support hold (assisted) | 2 × 10 sec | Feet on floor or low box | Rings set low. Arms straight, shoulders actively depressed (push down, not shrug). Feet take most of the weight. Assess shoulder control, scapular stability, and trunk stiffness. Note any asymmetry or shaking. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6HMUaAX2N0o | [ ] |

## Block 2 — Knee load screen (15 min)

*Only proceed if knee ≤ 3/10 after Block 1. If above, skip to Block 3.*

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Load | Cues | Demo | Done |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Wall sit | 2 × 20 sec | Bodyweight | Back flat against wall, thighs parallel or above parallel. Stop if pain increases during the hold. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pjbGm5EQd1s | [ ] |
| Step-up (vault or low beam) | 2 × 8 each leg | Bodyweight | Use the vault or low beam as your step — better than a generic box. Lead with right leg first. Drive through the heel. Observe knee tracking over toes. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=otjVTHFMbcM | [ ] |
| Slow bodyweight squat | 2 × 8 | Bodyweight | Feet shoulder-width, toes slightly out. Descend to comfortable depth only — do not chase depth. Note where knee pain starts (if at all). | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rsD77RCdnJk | [ ] |

## Block 3 — Diastasis baseline (10 min)

*Self-check before any loaded core work. This is assessment, not training.*

| Check | What to do | What to note | Demo | Done |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lying heel slide | On back, slowly slide one heel along floor. Note any doming or coning at midline. | Present / absent. Which side worse. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Er-Fl_poWDk | [ ] |
| Dead bug (single arm only) | On back, extend one arm overhead slowly. Does midline bulge? | Present / absent. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=g_BYB0R-4Ws | [ ] |
| Calf raise breathing | Standing. Exhale and rise onto toes. Any downward pressure felt? | Present / absent. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tp8rUfCi_oA | [ ] |

**Note:** Do not progress to loaded core work until diastasis status is clearer. Log observations carefully for the physio. Also flag the hollow body hold result from the warm-up.
