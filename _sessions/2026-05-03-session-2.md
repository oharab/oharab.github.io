# Session 2 — Saturday 3 May 2026

**Phase:** Rehab — glute chain and knee tracking  
**Goal:** Build on Session 1 baseline. Add single-leg glute bridge, consolidate clamshell and hip abduction, reintroduce step-up with closer attention to knee tracking. Retry ring support and bar hang. Begin light dead bug progression for core.  
**Duration:** 60–75 min  
**Stop if:** Knee pain > 3/10, any sharp or locking sensation, diastasis coning that you cannot control with bracing, or breathing distress

> **On intensity:** Session 1 felt like RPE 5–6 and you noted you could go harder. Volume is deliberately kept close to Session 1 here — the new patterns (single-leg bridge, dead bug arm+leg, slow-eccentric step-up) ARE the harder work. Don't add reps or sets on the day; let the new patterns carry the load.

## Block 1 — Glute & hip (25 min)

*Building on the Session 1 baseline. Same exercises, slightly higher intent — focus on quality and noticing left-right difference.*

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Load | Cues | Demo | Done |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Glute bridge (bilateral) | 2 × 10 | Bodyweight | Feet hip-width, drive through heels, 2 sec pause at top. This is now your warm-up for the single-leg version below — treat it as activation, not a workout. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=NXwR65L7I-s | [ ] |
| Single-leg glute bridge | 2 × 8 each side | Bodyweight | From the top of a bilateral bridge, extend one leg straight. Hold 2 sec. Lower with control. Do right side first — notice if the pelvis drops or rotates. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7vTnfE6oiXk | [ ] |
| Clamshell | 2 × 10 each side | Bodyweight or light band if available | Side-lying, feet together. Rotate top knee up without rolling the pelvis back — **hold 2 sec at the top before lowering**. The hold is the work. If the pelvis starts rolling, stop the set there rather than grinding through with bad form. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dD9bpkitnW0 | [ ] |
| Side-lying hip abduction | 2 × 10 each side | Bodyweight | Straight top leg, slight forward lean of the foot. No hip hike. **Hold 2 sec at the top**, then slow lower (2 sec). Same principle as the clamshell — stop the set if the pelvis starts rolling. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BSGho5DuaR0 | [ ] |

## Block 3 — Core (10 min)

*Diastasis-aware. Stop immediately if you feel doming or coning — do not push through it.*

| Exercise | Sets × Reps | Load | Cues | Demo | Done |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Dead bug — single arm only | 2 × 6 each side | Bodyweight | On back, knees bent to 90° in the air, arms pointing at ceiling. Slowly lower ONE arm overhead. The opposite arm stays pointing up. Lower back stays pressed to floor. You felt tension at the top of the gap last time — stop the arm before that tension builds. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Digors4ztBw | [ ] |
| Dead bug — arm + opposite leg | 2 × 5 each side | Bodyweight | Same start position. Extend one arm overhead and the OPPOSITE leg out straight simultaneously, slowly. Only go as far as you can without lower back lifting or doming at midline. This is new — be conservative on range. | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=wr_gd3yjzkk | [ ] |

**Note:** If the arm-only version causes doming today, skip the arm + leg version entirely and repeat arm-only next session.
